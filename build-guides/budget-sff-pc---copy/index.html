      Building a small form factor (SFF) PC doesn’t have to drain your wallet. With smart planning and careful component choices, you can put together a compact yet high-performance system that stays within your budget. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the top budget components for an SFF build in 2025 and share tips to save money without sacrificing performance.

      Why Build a Budget SFF PC?

      Small form factor PCs are ideal for gamers, content creators, and professionals who need a powerful machine in a portable package. Although SFF builds can sometimes be expensive due to specialized parts, focusing on value-driven components lets you create a sleek, compact system without overspending.

      1. The Case: The Heart of Your SFF Build

      Top Pick: Cooler Master MasterBox NR200

      • Price: $80–$100
      • Size: 18.25 x 7.28 x 14.72 inches
      • Features: Excellent airflow, supports GPUs up to 330mm, tool-less design

      The Cooler Master MasterBox NR200 is one of the best budget-friendly cases for SFF builds. It offers ample room for most components while maintaining a compact footprint, and its modular design makes it beginner-friendly.

      Buy on Amazon

      2. CPU: Balancing Performance and Cost

      Top Pick: AMD Ryzen 5 5600

      • Price: $130–$150
      • Cores/Threads: 6/12
      • TDP: 65W

      The AMD Ryzen 5 5600 is a budget powerhouse that delivers excellent gaming and multitasking performance. Its low power consumption makes it an ideal choice for SFF builds, and it pairs well with affordable AM4 motherboards.

      3. GPU: Compact and Affordable

      Top Pick: NVIDIA GTX 1660 Super

      • Price: $200–$250
      • VRAM: 6GB GDDR6
      • Length: 200mm

      The NVIDIA GTX 1660 Super is an excellent budget GPU for 1080p gaming. It’s compact, power-efficient, and readily available in the used market. For those looking for new options, consider the AMD Radeon RX 6600.

      Buy on Amazon

      4. Motherboard: Compact and Feature-Packed

      Top Pick: ASRock B550M-ITX/ac

      • Price: $120–$150
      • Form Factor: Mini-ITX
      • Features: PCIe 4.0, Wi-Fi 6, USB 3.2 Gen 2

      The ASRock B550M-ITX/ac is a budget-friendly Mini-ITX motherboard that packs all the essential features for an SFF build. It supports Ryzen 5000 series CPUs and offers a great range of connectivity options.

      5. RAM: Affordable and Reliable

      Top Pick: Corsair Vengeance LPX 16GB (2 x 8GB) DDR4-3200

      • Price: $40–$50
      • Speed: 3200MHz
      • Design: Low profile, perfect for SFF cases

      16GB of DDR4 RAM is ample for gaming and multitasking. The Corsair Vengeance LPX is both affordable and reliable, and its low-profile design ensures it fits seamlessly into compact builds.

      6. Storage: Fast and Budget-Friendly

      Top Pick: Western Digital Blue SN570 500GB NVMe SSD

      • Price: $40–$50
      • Speed: Up to 3500 MB/s
      • Form Factor: M.2 2280

      An NVMe SSD like the WD Blue SN570 offers blazing-fast boot times and quick game loading. For additional storage, consider adding a 1TB SATA SSD or HDD alongside it.

      7. Power Supply: Reliable and Compact

      Top Pick: EVGA SuperNOVA 550 GM

      • Price: $80–$100
      • Wattage: 550W
      • Form Factor: SFX

      A dependable SFX power supply is crucial for SFF builds. The EVGA SuperNOVA 550 GM provides excellent efficiency and comes with a 7-year warranty, ensuring reliability over time.

      8. Cooling: Keeping It Cool on a Budget

      Top Pick: Noctua NH-L9a-AM4

      • Price: $50–$60
      • Height: 37mm
      • Compatibility: AM4

      The Noctua NH-L9a-AM4 is a low-profile cooler designed for tight spaces. It runs quietly and efficiently, ensuring your CPU stays cool even under heavy loads.

      Tips for Building a Budget SFF PC

      • Buy Used Components: Look for deals on GPUs, cases, and power supplies on platforms like eBay or local marketplaces.
      • Prioritize Airflow: Choose a case with excellent ventilation to prevent overheating.
      • Plan Your Layout: Measure your components and plan cable routing before starting your build.
      • Check Compatibility: Use tools like PCPartPicker to ensure all parts work together seamlessly.

      Conclusion

      Building a budget SFF PC is all about finding the right balance between performance, size, and cost. With the components outlined above, you can assemble a compact and powerful system for under $800. Whether you’re gaming, editing, or just browsing, a budget SFF PC offers a great way to enjoy high performance without breaking the bank.
